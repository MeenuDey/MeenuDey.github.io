Meenu Rani Dey

Dr. Meenu Rani Dey completed her Ph.D. in Computer Science and Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Guwahati in 2025. She earned her M.Tech degree from IIIT Bhubaneswar and her B.Tech from CSVTU, Chhattisgarh. Dr. Dey's research interests lie at the intersection of network security, 5G-V2X communication, and ML-based attack detection. During her doctoral studies, she focused on securing Cellular Vehicle-to-Everything (C-V2X) networks through efficient real-time detection and localization of DoS attacks, Sybil attack detection via trust-based clustering, and federated learning frameworks for unknown attack identification. Her research has been published in prestigious journals such as IEEE Transactions on Vehicular Technology and IEEE Transactions on Intelligent Transportation Systems, and presented at reputed conferences including IEEE DATE, COMSNETS, and ACM MobiCom, among others. Beyond research, Dr. Dey has actively contributed to the academic community as a TPC Member and Reviewer for IEEE ICC, a Reviewer for Springer JNSM, and a Session Chair at Indocrypt 2025. She has also served on the organising committees of several national and international events including COMSNETS, AIMLSystems, and Indocrypt. She currently serves as an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at IIIT Bhubaneswar.
